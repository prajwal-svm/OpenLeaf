\section{Citing sources}
natbib gives you flexible citation commands. Use \citet{turing1950} when the
author is part of the sentence, and a parenthetical citation at the end of a claim
\citep{shannon1948}. You can cite several works at once
\citep{turing1950,shannon1948,knuth1984}, add a prefix
\citep[see][]{knuth1984}, or cite a page \citep[p.~42]{shannon1948}.

Switch the look of the citations and the reference list by changing the
bibliography style (for example \texttt{plainnat}, \texttt{abbrvnat}, or a journal
style). Oleafly runs BibTeX for you automatically.

\section{Adding entries}
Add references to \texttt{refs.bib} as \texttt{@article}, \texttt{@book},
\texttt{@inproceedings}, and so on. Oleafly can also fetch entries for you from a
DOI, arXiv id, or title (see Add citation).
